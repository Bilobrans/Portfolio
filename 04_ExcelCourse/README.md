### **Situation**
The Junior Enterprise identified that many students and professionals struggled with **basic Excel concepts**, such as data formatting, formulas, and chart creation ‚Äî skills essential for academic and professional success.  
To address this, I developed and taught a **beginner-friendly Excel course** to build a solid foundation for these participants.

### **Task**
My main responsibilities were to:
- **Design and teach** a complete introductory Excel course;  
- Develop **step-by-step exercises** for immediate practice;  
- Ensure participants understood and could apply basic concepts independently;

### **Action**
The course was divided into progressive modules to build understanding step by step:

1. **üíª Interface and Navigation**

2. **üé® Formatting and Conditional Formatting**

3. **üßÆ Basic Formulas**

   - Practical exercises to apply formulas in real scenarios.

4. **üîç Filters and Data Organization**
   - Use of filters and sorting tools to manage large datasets.

5. **üìä Charts and Visualization**

   - Emphasis on how to choose the right chart for each situation.

The methodology combined **short explanations**, **immediate practice**, and **realistic examples** to help participants feel confident using Excel from the very first lesson.

### **Result**
The minicourse generated excellent results for both the participants and the Junior Enterprise:

- Students and professionals gained **confidence in using Excel** for academic and work-related tasks.

- The use of **conditional formatting, filters, and charts** improved their ability to analyze and communicate results.  
- Several participants reported that even though the course was introductory, they learned many things they didn't know.
